# 📊 Customer Segmentation using RFM Analysis

A data analytics project that uses RFM (Recency, Frequency, Monetary) Analysis to understand customer behavior, identify high-value segments, and generate actionable business insights.

---

## 📌 Project Overview
This project analyzes customer transaction data to segment customers based on their purchasing behavior. It helps businesses identify valuable customers, improve retention strategies, and optimize marketing efforts.

---

## ❓ Problem Statement
Businesses often struggle to identify their most valuable customers and effectively target them. This project solves that problem using RFM-based customer segmentation.

---

## 🎯 Objectives
- Analyze customer purchase behavior  
- Segment customers into meaningful groups  
- Identify high-value and at-risk customers  
- Generate insights for business decision-making  

---

## 🧠 What is RFM Analysis?

- **Recency (R):** How recently a customer made a purchase  
- **Frequency (F):** How often a customer purchases  
- **Monetary (M):** How much money a customer spends  

Customers are scored from 1 to 5 and grouped into segments.

---

## ⚙️ Methodology

### 1. Data Cleaning
- Handled missing values  
- Removed duplicates  
- Converted date columns into proper format  

### 2. RFM Calculation
- Calculated Recency, Frequency, Monetary values  
- Created RFM table for each customer  

### 3. RFM Scoring
- Assigned scores (1–5) using quantiles  

### 4. Customer Segmentation
Customers were segmented into:
- 🏆 Champions  
- 💎 Loyal Customers  
- 🌱 Potential Loyalists  
- ⚠️ At Risk  
- 💤 Lost Customers  

---

## 📊 Key Insights
- Top 20% customers contribute ~80% of total revenue (Pareto Principle)  
- Loyal customers form the largest segment  
- Champions generate the highest revenue  
- At-risk customers indicate potential churn

## 💡 Business Impact
- Helps target high-value customers  
- Improves customer retention strategies  
- Enables data-driven marketing decisions
